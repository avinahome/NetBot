Firstly, it is the correct pronunciation of the first syllable my last name, Avina(æ-vin-à). A shortcut explanation of which my family has mentally and socially mined for, unrelentingly, just in my lifetime with: “It rhymes with Avenue”; “It sounds kinda like ‘Havin’ a’.” and; my favorite, “Rhymes with Banana!”. @, the atpersand, though, is a single syllable with two parts, one from the back and one from the front of the mouth. It is the foremost representation of the internet, both online and off. Born of email and adopted by twitter and insta, it has, spoken or linked, represented to the world our online presence. Now, in this new system, it will have the ability to alter our presence online or off. If you say or type @ and then anything after it, it can be, now, a linkable routine or other type of command, idea, word or data, within a fully encrypted contained environment.

jimini could break down the vocalizations or other symbolic input, then repurpose them as a user’s personal commands following confirmed vocal recognition or other UserID. These parts can then be rebuilt in qubits and arranged with great speed. The result is an infinitely linkable, contained and enclosed but expandable and encryptable environment based on numbers yes, as with computers already, but more completely, able to freely associate words and ideas, links, and possibly even memes once underway.

8. Why the .(dot/Full stop/period)?

Humans have a tough time not having spaces between words. Computers don’t NEED them.

Thissentence | this sentence | this.sentence | this/sentence

These all look pretty similar to Google’s search engine in the end, but have different meanings to us. The / example is only necessary because of spaces in between words. Space, otherwise defined. While we could offer the user an option to have the computer guess at the words and add spaces if it does not already contain them, as long as it maintains a very high accuracy, blockchain size and efficiency would suggest that we not use both dots AND spaces behind the scenes. Studies have suggested that even the order of letters is less important than we think. So may be spaces. Phrases short (.@.) or long (zyx...@...xyz) or very long, as in an entire book or encyclopedia, is stored as is, and “thought of” by jimini both as individual pieces, and as a whole.
